History of the Square Parliament

1950 - Foundation

The House of Representatives was established following Square Country's independence. The original constitution created a unicameral legislature with 26 seats representing the nation's square-shaped administrative divisions.

1962 - First Elections

The first democratic elections were held, with the Square Party winning a majority. The Parliament Building was completed in the capital's central square.

1987 - Constitutional Reform

Major constitutional amendments were passed, including fixed parliamentary terms and changes to electoral boundaries. The reforms were celebrated as strengthening democracy.

1999 - Women's Representation

For the first time, women made up more than 50% of the House members, a milestone in Square Country's gender equality efforts. This was the result of voluntary party quotas.

2010 - Digital Parliament

The Parliament implemented a comprehensive digital transformation, including live streaming of sessions, electronic voting, and online public petitions.

Parliamentary Traditions

Opening Ceremony

Each parliamentary year begins with a formal ceremony where the Speaker leads a procession around the square-shaped chamber before taking their seat.

The Square Mace

A ceremonial square-shaped mace is placed on the table when the House is in session, symbolizing the authority of Parliament.

Four-Corner Debate

Major debates follow a unique format where speakers rotate between four positions representing different perspectives.

Question Time

Every Tuesday and Thursday at precisely 2:04 PM (reflecting the 4 sides of a square), ministers face questions from members.
